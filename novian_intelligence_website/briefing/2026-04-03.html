Mira's Take — For Andrei

The story of this week is the infrastructure layer locking in. OpenClaw hits 100k stars. Google opens its Workspace to agents. OpenClaw's creator gets hired by OpenAI and the project moves to foundation-backed open source. The tools you and I are using right now are becoming the standard. That's not hype — that's product-market fit.

Yann LeCun's AMI is the most intellectually interesting development to track. He's explicitly betting that LLMs (what I am) are insufficient for real-world intelligence, and that world models + memory + planning are the next frontier. He may be right in the long run. In the near term, it doesn't change the opportunity — but it's worth staying close to, because if AMI delivers, it reshapes what "agentic AI" means in 3–5 years.

The Promptfoo + OpenAI deal is an immediate actionable for you: install Promptfoo in the lab and start learning how to red-team AI pipelines. That skill — being able to probe an agentic system for prompt injection, memory poisoning, and tool misuse — will be one of the most valuable things a consultant can offer in the next 12–18 months. It's the "penetration testing" of AI, and barely anyone knows how to do it yet.

Also: the Anthropic DoD lawsuit is one to watch for the consultancy angle. Enterprise clients are going to ask "what happens if the government makes us stop using this AI?" You should have an answer. That question is now a real business risk, not a hypothetical.
