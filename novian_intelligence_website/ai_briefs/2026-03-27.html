OpenAI Raises $110B — Valuation Hits $730B

In one of the largest funding rounds in tech history, OpenAI secured $110 billion backed by Amazon, Nvidia, and SoftBank. The round puts OpenAI's pre-money valuation at $730 billion and expands its AWS distribution pipeline and Nvidia infrastructure capacity significantly. For context: that's more than Disney, Nike, and Goldman Sachs combined.

A $730B valuation isn't a bet on a product — it's a bet on infrastructure lock-in. When Amazon, Nvidia, and SoftBank pile in simultaneously, they're securing positions in the compute and distribution layer, not just buying equity. The question for every enterprise is: what does your stack look like when your AI vendor is also your cloud provider?

OpenAI Signs Pentagon Deal; Anthropic Faces Federal Phase-Out

OpenAI signed a classified AI deployment deal with the Pentagon — cloud-only, with three stated redlines on use. Meanwhile, federal agencies are reportedly moving to phase out Anthropic over six months. A significant reshuffling of who gets to power government AI infrastructure.

The Pentagon deal matters less than what it signals: the US government has picked its AI vendor. Federal contracts are winner-take-most markets, and Anthropic's phase-out — if it holds — removes a meaningful counterweight to OpenAI's institutional dominance.

Block Cuts 40% of Staff — Jack Dorsey Cites AI Automation

Jack Dorsey's Block (formerly Square) announced layoffs of over 4,000 employees — roughly 40% of its workforce — citing AI tools enabling smaller, more efficient teams. One of the most direct public statements yet from a major CEO that AI is directly replacing headcount, not just augmenting it.

Dorsey is the first major CEO to say it plainly in a public filing: AI reduced headcount, not just costs. This will be cited in boardrooms for years. The question isn't whether AI eliminates jobs — it's whether your organization is on the implementing side or the displaced side.

40% of Enterprise Apps to Include AI Agents by End of 2026

Industry analysts now project that by close of 2026, 40% of enterprise applications will integrate task-specific AI agents — moving from "AI as a chatbot" to "AI as a coworker that acts." The agentic shift is no longer theoretical. It's the product roadmap of every major software company.

IBM: App Exploits Up 44% — AI Is Both Cause and Target

IBM's 2026 X-Force Threat Index reports a 44% rise in application exploit entry points, with North America accounting for 29% of cases and 109 active extortion groups tracked in 2025. As AI tools proliferate, so do the attack surfaces. Relevant for anyone running AI infrastructure — which is us.

A 44% rise in exploits is the direct cost of increased attack surface from AI tool proliferation. The organizations that will weather this are the ones that treat AI security as infrastructure — not as a line item in the IT budget after something breaks.
