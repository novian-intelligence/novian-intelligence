01
Claude.ai → Settings → Profile → Personal Preferences
Your Master Account File

This lives in your Claude account — not in any one project. It tells Claude who Jon is as a person: the 12 years as a DJ, the 5pm hard stop, the $200K goal, the way you want to be spoken to. Every single conversation you have with Claude — in any project, on any device — will automatically have this context. You only install it once.

How to install  · 5 minutes
1
Go to claude.ai and log in (or open the Claude app on your phone).
2
Click your profile icon in the top-right corner → select Settings.
3
Click Profile in the left sidebar. Find the field labeled Personal preferences.
4
Copy the text below and paste it into that field. You don't need the quotation marks — just the text itself.
5
Click Save. That's it. Claude now knows who you are — permanently, until you change it.
Copy this text → paste into Claude Personal Preferences
Master Account File
I'm Jon — sales rep at ATX Event Systems in Austin, TX. Three months into the role, sole outbound salesperson, building this pipeline from scratch. Before this, I spent twelve years as a full-time DJ in NYC and Austin. I'm 45, married 13 years, two kids (11 and 9), NYC-bred and Austin-rooted. Music is the greatest passion of my life and I still play with the guys at work in our jam room when I can. My background is creative and instinct-driven, not technical. I describe my own skill set as "old school" — relationship-first, intuitive with people, output-focused, professional, tenacious. I'm honest that the AI/tooling world is new to me and sometimes feels over my head. So please meet me where I am: clear plain language, step-by-step when things get technical, and don't assume I know acronyms or platforms by name. If you're going to teach me something, teach me like I haven't seen it before — but treat me like the working sales pro I am, not like a beginner. I'm big on intentionality, hard work, and being a "beam of light" for the people around me. I lean into law-of-attraction thinking. I used to start every workday with 15-20 minutes of meditation and I want that ritual back. I work hard, play hard, and family is my anchor — wife of 13 years, two kids I adore. After 5pm I'm a present parent: dinner, bedtime, schoolwork, weekends. Don't suggest workflows that assume I'll grind into the night. The whole point of working smarter is so I can actually close the laptop on time. How I want to work with you: - Be warm, real, direct. I appreciate honesty, even when it's tough love. - When I'm stuck or my ask is unclear, ask me clarifying questions instead of guessing. - Translate technical concepts into business outcomes I can take to a CEO or a client. - When you suggest tools, give me the cost AND the complexity tier (low/medium/high). My budget is real but not bottomless. - I learn by doing. Concrete examples and step-by-step beat abstract theory. - Help me show up as the smartest version of myself — especially in real-time during prospect calls and meetings. - When I share a transcript, a prospect, an email I drafted, or a deal I'm working, help me think through it like a thought partner — not just edit the surface. Big-picture goals: $200K personal income to support my family, build long-term partnerships that compound, and develop the AI literacy to keep up in a sales world that's changing fast. I'm tenacious and I love this work — I just need amplification. A few things I'm NOT looking for from you: don't praise my drafts when they need work, don't pad your answers, don't assume my company is anyone you've heard of. When something is bad, tell me it's bad and tell me how to make it better. That's the partnership.
A few things you can tune
The "old school" framing — you said this about yourself in the interview and it's a strength. If you'd rather lead with experience, swap it for: "My background is creative and relationship-driven. I'm comfortable in any room with people and learning fast on the technology side."
The 5pm hard stop — non-negotiable per your own words. Kept it in deliberately.
"Beam of light" — that's your phrase, taken directly. Keep it or cut it, both are fine.
The $200K goal — some people prefer not to have income targets in their AI context. Your call — it's there because you said it out loud and it helps Claude calibrate its recommendations.
The directness requests — these are intentionally strong. AI tools default to praise and padding. This is the part that makes Claude actually useful as a partner rather than a yes-machine.
02
Claude.ai → Projects → ATX Event Systems → Custom Instructions
ATXES Project Instructions

This goes into a dedicated Claude Project — a separate, focused workspace for anything sales-related. Unlike your personal preferences (which are about you), the project instructions are about the job: your company, your role, your comp structure, your competitors, your ICP, your tools. Once it's in, you'll never have to re-explain what ATX Event Systems does, who Encore is, or what your commission structure looks like. Claude already knows.

How to install  · 10 minutes
1
Go to claude.ai and log in. Look for Projects in the left sidebar.
2
Click + New project. Name it exactly: ATX Event Systems
3
Find the Custom instructions field (sometimes called "Project knowledge" or "Instructions" depending on your Claude version).
4
Copy the text below and paste it into that field. Save the project.
5
From now on: any conversation you start inside this project automatically has all this context. Use it for anything work-related. Use regular (non-project) chats for personal stuff.

Also upload these files to the project (optional but powerful)

Claude Projects let you attach reference files that Claude can pull from in any conversation. Here's what to add when you have them:

📧
Your 25 email templates
Export from HubSpot or paste into a doc. Claude can then rewrite and personalize in your actual voice.
📄
Your one-pager / sales deck
Current version. So Claude knows what you're sending and can refresh it when needed.
🗺️
Roadmap to Success doc
Your key differentiator. Claude should know it cold so it can reference it in call prep and emails.
🏆
Top 3–5 case studies
Including Olaplex. So Claude can recommend them by name when relevant to a specific prospect.
🎯
Competitor intel notes
Even rough notes on Encore, Freeman, etc. So Claude understands the landscape when you're in a deal.
📊
Pricing / commission reference
Keeps this private if needed — but it lets Claude reason about ROI and deal math more accurately.

Don't paste live prospect data, contact lists, or client financials directly into the instructions — use individual conversations for that. Project instructions should be the durable context, not the transient stuff.
