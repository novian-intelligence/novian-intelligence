Key Competitive Intelligence
The Encore Problem — and How Jon Wins Around It
Encore (Jon's biggest competitor) wins on venue exclusivity and scale, not quality. They've locked in multi-year agreements at major hotels and convention centers, forcing event planners to use them or pay to break out. The strategic counter:

1. Get there first. Reach out to conference organizers 6–12 months before their event — before they sign the venue contract that locks in Encore. Jon's convention calendar approach is right; it needs to be automated and scaled.

2. Capture the disgruntled. Event planners and corporations who've been burned by Encore's "bare minimum" service are actively looking for alternatives. Position ATX ES's "Roadmap to Success" and white-glove service as the antidote. Monitor LinkedIn for event planners complaining about in-house AV.

3. Venue partnerships before Encore gets there. Parkside Projects (Brazos Hall, 800 Congress) is already in progress. Add 10 more Austin venues. Exclusive or preferred vendor status is exactly how Encore built its moat — Jon can do the same at the boutique level.
