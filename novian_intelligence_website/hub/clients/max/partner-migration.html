Tracking Transition Strategy

Highest Technical Risk

The Single Most Dangerous Part of Any Affiliate Migration

Tracking cutover is where migrations silently fail. A partner's link stops attributing, they don't notice for a week, and suddenly you owe them commissions you can't prove. The decision of how you cut over — hard or dual — has massive downstream consequences for data integrity, partner trust, and finance reconciliation.

❌ Option A — Hard Cutover

Impact tracking disabled on March 31. PartnerStack goes live the same day. No overlap.

  • Risk: Any tracking misconfiguration goes undetected until partner revenue drops
  • Risk: No validation window — you're flying blind on Day 1
  • Risk: Parameter mismatches in Snowflake/Tableau won't surface until reporting is wrong
  • Upside: Simpler operationally — one system, one source of truth
  • Upside: No deduplication headaches between two platforms

Given the timeline constraints, this may be the reality for March 31 — but mitigate with Wave 0 testing.

✅ Option B — Dual Tracking (Recommended)

Both Impact and PartnerStack fire simultaneously for a validation period. Compare attribution in real time.

Affiliate Link → Your Website
    ├── Impact fires (existing)
    └── PartnerStack fires (new)
Compare both → validate → cut Impact when PS numbers match
  • Catch parameter mismatches before they cost anyone money
  • Validate Snowflake pipeline is receiving correct PS data
  • Build partner confidence — "we confirmed your links are tracking"
  • Gives Max evidence to show Mark that the migration is working
⚠ Decision Required — Max + Engineering

Given the hard March 31 Impact cutoff, true dual tracking may not be possible for the full deadline window. Minimum viable version: run dual tracking for the Critical 10 partners during Days 6–10 as a validation layer before mass migration. Confirm with engineering if Impact's tracking can stay active in parallel while PS is being tested.

Wave 0 — Internal Test (NEW)

Before ANY partner outreach, run a real internal test wave with 5–10 friendly/internal users who will tolerate bugs and give feedback.

  • Use internal team members or trusted contacts as test "affiliates"
  • Generate test tracking links in PartnerStack
  • Run test conversions (CPC, CPA, CPL) and verify all three hit correctly
  • Confirm data flows through to Snowflake and appears in Tableau
  • Only proceed to Wave 1 outreach after Wave 0 passes

Validation Checklist (Per Partner)

  • New PS link generated and functional
  • Test click registers in PS dashboard
  • Test conversion registers with correct commission type
  • Partner ID appears correctly in Snowflake
  • Commission amount matches contracted rate
  • Partner can see activity in their PS dashboard

Historical Data Decision

Partners will ask: "Can I see my past performance?" This requires a decision before launch.

Option 1 — Start Fresh

PS shows only post-migration data. Partners reference Impact for history during the 2-month window. Simpler but partners may feel data loss.

Option 2 — Export Summary CSV

Export each partner's lifetime stats from Impact and email as a personal performance summary. Low tech, high-touch, appreciated. Recommended for T1.

Link Replacement Strategy

Partner Experience Risk

The Silent Revenue Leak

Partners don't just have one link in one place. They have old Impact links embedded across dozens of touchpoints — many of which they've forgotten about. After March 31st, those links go dark. Every unupdated link = lost attribution = partner dispute. The plan needs to address not just "tell partners to update" but where links hide and what happens to the old ones.

Where Old Impact Links Are Hiding

Partners need to audit and update all of these locations:

  • Blog posts & articles — often dozens, possibly hundreds of embedded links
  • YouTube video descriptions — old videos stay live indefinitely
  • Email newsletters & sequences — automated drips may have hardcoded links
  • Coupon & deal sites — aggregator sites rarely update proactively
  • Paid ad campaigns — destination URLs in Google/Meta ads
  • Partner landing pages — dedicated review or promo pages
  • Social media bios & posts — link-in-bio tools, pinned posts
  • Browser bookmarks / internal tools — less common but worth noting

Include a Link Audit Prompt in Partner Email

  • Ask partners to search their own content for your brand name + "impact.com" to surface old links
  • Claude can write this as a checklist to attach to the migration email

The 301 Redirect Option

The gold standard for link migrations: set up server-side redirects so old Impact links automatically forward to new PartnerStack links. This means partners don't have to update — their old links keep working.

impact.com/affiliate?id=1234
    → 301 redirect (your server)
        partnerstack.com/ref/newid
  • Best case: Engineering builds a redirect layer — old links never break, attribution continues seamlessly
  • Realistic case: Impact controls the old link domain, so true 301s may not be possible
  • Minimum: Confirm with Impact — do old tracking links return a 404 or redirect somewhere after March 31?
⚠ Engineering Must Clarify

What happens to Impact tracking links after March 31st? Do they 404, redirect to homepage, or stay dormant? This determines urgency of partner link updates. If Impact links 404 immediately, partners lose traffic — not just attribution.

Partner ID Mapping Table

A simple but critical artifact. Every partner needs a row mapping their Impact ID to their new PartnerStack ID. Without this, support tickets, finance audits, and attribution reconciliation become a nightmare. Build this as part of the CSV process.

Rollback Plan

Break-Glass Scenario

Hope for the best. Plan for the worst.

Most migrations don't need a rollback. But every migration plan that doesn't have one leaves leadership nervous. Having this written down costs Max 10 minutes and buys enormous confidence. If PartnerStack tracking fails silently after cutover, the business needs a clear, pre-agreed procedure — not a panicked group chat at midnight on March 31st.

🚨 Trigger Conditions

Execute rollback if ANY of the following occur post-launch:

  • PartnerStack tracking shows zero conversions for 24+ hours when traffic is confirmed active
  • Snowflake pipeline shows no data flowing from PartnerStack
  • 2+ Critical 10 partners report attribution discrepancies
  • Commission calculations are materially wrong vs contracted rates
  • PartnerStack platform outage lasting more than 4 hours during business hours

Rollback Procedure

  • Step 1: Notify Mark immediately — do not troubleshoot alone
  • Step 2: Contact PartnerStack CSM/support — get them on a call within the hour
  • Step 3: Confirm with engineering whether Impact tracking can be re-enabled (confirm this is even possible BEFORE March 31)
  • Step 4: Send partner communication: "brief technical delay, your Impact links remain active"
  • Step 5: Do NOT ask partners to revert their links unless truly necessary — that creates double work
  • Step 6: Resume migration after root cause is identified and resolved

Pre-Rollback Prep (Do Before March 31)

  • Confirm with Impact: can tracking be re-enabled after cutover if needed, and for how long?
  • Save all PartnerStack CSM + support contact details somewhere accessible offline
  • Have engineering team available (not on vacation) the week of March 31st
  • Document rollback decision-maker: who has authority to call it — Max alone, or Mark must approve?
